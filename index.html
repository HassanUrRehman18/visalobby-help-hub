FAQS

Which visa do I need to visit my chosen country?
There are numerous options of visas available for travellers. It may easily confuse them if it's their first time travelling abroad. If you or your loved ones are about to prepare for an abroad visit, then go to www.visalobby.com to learn which visa type you may need. Also, what documents do you require for it, the application process and more...
(UK) Can I bring my family to the UK on a Tier 4 Student Visa?
Yes. In most cases, you are allowed to sponsor your family (parents, wife or kids) to join you in the UK. However, there are exceptions that apply to some degree levels. Before applying for a visa, you should find this information on the UKBA website or ask your visa consultant.
(UK) What if I need more time to complete my studies?
Generally a student visa ends a couple of months after the course completion date to allow you to make further plans for progression. However, if more time is required to complete your studies, you can extend your visa in certain circumstances or you may be issued a Short-term Study Visa. You should discuss this with your University and contact a Visa Consultant for processing details.
My visa was damaged. What should I do?
Visa is the legal document that proves your right to stay in a country. If your visa has been damaged in any way, you must contact the visa issuing authority immediately. Most countries reissue a visa stamp for a fee.
(UK) Can I work in the UK with a student visa?
Yes. UK Student Visa allows you to work upto 20 hours per week - depending upon the level of degree you are enrolled in.
(UK) Do I have to register with the UK police?
Depending on your nationality, you may be required to register with the police when you arrive in the UK. You need to do this within seven days of your arrival in the UK.
(UK) What do I need to apply for admission at a UK University?
General requirements are your academic certificates, transcripts, passport copy, CV, reference letters, work experience letters (if any), IELTS (if required) and other related documents.
(UK) What is the visa application processing time? Can I track progress?
